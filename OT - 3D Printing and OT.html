3D Printing and Ornamental Turning

3D Printing has opened up some very interesting and cost-effective options with ornamental turning. Additionally, complex shapes can be modeled in a 3D CAD program like Fusion 360, and then made quite perfectly.

  • Certain items like rubbers with special shapes (like these for the MDF Rose Engine Lathe) will not be used frequently and make sense to 3D print.

    • Different shapes for rubbing the rosettes can be quickly mocked up and tested.

    • 3D printing filaments like PLA are softer than the materials used in most rosettes, so they are unlikely to damage the rosette(s).

  • Rosettes are area where there seems to be some emerging thought.

    • Colin Spencer made a Home-made Rose Engine lathe (with a number of 3D printed parts), and he also 3D prints his Rosettes. He has been kind enough to publish the designs for others to use.

    • Ian Lane published an article in the Spring 2022 "SOT Bulletin" titled, "Rosette Design and Fabrication". That article outlines his use of OpenSCAD to design a rosette for 3D printing.

  • Other items like gears and pulleys are also viable for 3D printing. Some may necessitate other filament types.
