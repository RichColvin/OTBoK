Design Standards

The Open Rose Engine Lathe is designed around these criteria.

Overall Ideas

  1. Easily available, off-the-shelf parts are to be used as much as possible.

  2. Where parts must be made, they need to be easily made by a person with reasonable machining skills. (this probably needs more definition)

  3. Where parts must be made, consideration shall be designed into the part which enables them to be 3D printed using PLA or similar materials.

  4. The lathe's base will be made using 80/20 T-slot extruded aluminum.

    Lathe Axes
    (click on image to see bigger versions)
  1. A stepper motor will be used to drive the spindle.

  2. Stepper motors will be used to drive movement on the Z, X, and B axes.

  3. The Control System for Multiple Stepper Motors will be used for the controls.

    (This is the same as was used on the MDF Rose Engine Lathe 2.0.)

Spindle

  1. Spindle diameter is 0.995" ± 0.002".

  2. Spindle nose (the inboard side) shall provide for work-holding using one of these formats:
    1. Morse taper #2
      1. Drawbar used shall be 3/8"-16.
    2. ER collet chuck
      1. ER-25 or
      2. ER-32
    3. Threaded, 1"-8.

  3. Spindle rotation shall be designed for a maximum of 15 revolutions per minute (RPM).

  4. Spindle shall be held using either
    1. linear bearings,
    2. ball or roller bearings, or
    3. self-lubricating bushings.

    Spindle-Pulley
    (click on image to see bigger versions)
  5. Spindle shall be rotated using a drive pulley. A 3D-printable version is available on GitHub at https://github.com/elfren/RoseEngine_SpindleAndAxis/tree/master/Hardware/GT2-5mm-108t.
    1. Diameter of the drive pulley shall be approximately 7" (as outlined in the diagram to the right).
    2. Thickness of the drive pulley shall be 3/4".
    3. The drive pulley shall be GT2 (5mm) pitch, enabling it to be driven using a timing belt.

  6. The drive pulley shall be affixed to the spindle shaft using a flange.
    1. The flange may be either fixed, or provision for crossing wheel functionality to allow for using a screw to change the angular alignment.
    2. In either case, the the flange shall be 1" in thickness.

Rosettes

  1. The spindle shall be capable of utilizing 1, 2, or 3 rosettes:
    1. Up to 2 rosettes for rocking, and
    2. Up to 1 rosette for pumping.

  2. Rosettes shall have a nominal diameter of 7".

  3. Rosettes shall have a spindle diameter of 1.00".

Lathe Headstock

  1. Distance between the centerlines for the headstock's pivot point and the spindle shall be 12".

  2. Spindle Rocking
    1. Spindle shall be capable of being rocked in an arc of 5° (2.5° to each side of a line drawn vertically from the pivot point).

    2. Spindle rocking shall be accomplished by affixing the headstock to pillow blocks using a 1/2" shaft. These pillow blocks are attached to the lathe bed.

  3. Spindle Pumping
    1. Spindle shall be capable of being pumped with the lateral movement (along the Z axis) up to 1/4".

    2. Spindle pumping shall be accomplished by moving the headstock along the bottom shaft. Such movement shall be accomplished by using linear bearings in the pillow blocks.

    3. A spring shall be used to position the headstock towards the outboard side, and pumping shall move the headstock towards the inboard side.

Lathe Bed

  1. Bed shall be metallic and capable of having items secured to it using magnetic switches.

  2. Bed shall be at least 24" x 24".

  3. The bed will be designed so that it can be moved away from the headstock, providing a gap-bed configuration.

Amplitude Adjuster
(click on image to see bigger versions)
Rubber Support for Rocking
  1. Vertical amplitude adjusters shall be provisioned so that they are used whenever performing rocking activities.

  2. Vertical amplitude adjusters shall be designed to be used on either the near or far side of the headstock.

  3. Amplitude adjusters shall be designed so that there are separate lever arms for each of the two rosettes.

  4. The lever arms shall be long enough to provide for amplitude adjustment up to 125%.

  5. The AA rubbers (shown in red in the drawing to the right) used for each lever arm shall be independently adjustable, allowing for differing engagement of the rosette rubbers.

  6. The AA rubbers shall be adjustable up or down for the full length of the lever arm.

  7. The rosette rubbers (shown in blue in the drawing to the right) shall be printable using a 3D-printer, enabling the ornamental turner to create their own designs as desired.

  8. Guides shall be provided for the lever arms to ensure the rosette rubbers stay engaged with their respective rosettes.

Stepper Motors

  1. GX-16/4 Connectors
    GX Plug GX Jack
    Stepper motors are connected to the associated control mechanism using 16mm, GX/16-4 connectors.

    GX-style connectors are available at a reasonable cost from many sources, and they are threaded so that the plug has little chance of being pulled out of the jack unintentionally.


  2. The cable connections are

    GX-16/4
    Plug
    Pin     		Stepper
    Wire     		Control
    Board     		GX-16/4
    Jack
    Pin
    1 A+ ⇔ A+ 1
    2 A- ⇔ A- 2
    3 B+ ⇔ B+ 3
    4 B- ⇔ B- 4

  3. GX-16 plugs are used on the stepper motor connector cables.

  4. GX-16 jacks are used on the controls box.

HMI Display

    GX-12/4 Connectors
    GX Plug GX Jack
  1. The HMI display (when appropriate) is connected to the associated control mechanism using 12mm, GX/12-4 connectors.

    These are a different size than the stepper motor connector to prevent the user connecting the HMI into a stepper motor jack.


  2. The cable connections are

    GX-12/4
    Plug
    Pin     		HMI
    Wire     		Control
    Board     		GX-12/4
    Jack
    Pin
    1 Rx ⇔ Tx 1
    2 +5V ⇔ +5V 2
    3 Tx ⇔ Rx 3
    4 GND ⇔ GND 4

  3. GX-12 plugs are used on the HMI display connector cable.

  4. GX-12 jacks are used on the controls box.

Limit Switches

    3.5mm Audo Connectors
    3.5mm Plug 3.5mm Jack
  1. Limit switches used are normally open/momentary close. When closed, the designated pin is shorted to GND.

  2. 3.5mm audio plugs are used on the connector cable from the limit switch.

  3. 3.5mm audio jacks are used on the controls box.
