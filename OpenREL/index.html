The Open Rose Engine Lathe is an open-source engineering design, enabling the building of a rose engine lathe to an open standard. The intent of this project is to:

  • enable the ornamental turner to build their system to an accepted standard,

  • enable the development of suppliers of ornamental turning equipment outside the USA by enabling them to supply standardized parts,

  • enable standardized training approaches, and

  • allow for the continued growth of ornamental turning by making the acquisition of a rose engine lathe less costly.

The design is open for use by any company to manufacture these lathes or parts for them. It is licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International License.

There are two goals with this endeavor:

  1. The current community of users of rose engine lathes is not the youth of today. We need to make it easy for new users to get a machine at a reasonable cost, and

  2. The systems used for any levels of automation are dependent on key individuals who will not be in such a support mode one day. We need the ability to keep things moving when they are no longer providing such great efforts.
